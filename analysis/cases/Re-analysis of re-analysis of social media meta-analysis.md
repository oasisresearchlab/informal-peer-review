**In the preprint:** Yes — Patel & Chan (2026), §6.1.2 *Re-analysis of social media meta-analysis* (RQ1 — Who, Case 2).

<span style="color:orange">Ferguson's (2024) meta-analysis concluded that social media's impacts on adolescent mental health were "nonsignificant." Psychologist Jonathan Haidt and colleague Zach Rausch re-analyzed the data to push back; Ph.D. student Matthew Jané then critiqued the rigor of *their* re-analysis and published his own on his blog, finding a null effect of social-media abstinence on self-reported teen mental health for 0–10-week interventions. Haidt and Jané conversed directly across Twitter, Bluesky, and blog posts; both admitted errors, and Jané's work evolved into a living meta-analysis updated as new data arrive. The longitudinal, cross-community case went viral and reached *The Conversation*.</span>[^claude-case]
