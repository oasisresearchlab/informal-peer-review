<span style="color:orange">One of the study's longest and most publicized cases: interdisciplinary scientist Joe Bak-Coleman — who had also been a formal reviewer of the paper — worked with other reviewers over years to critique Protzko et al.'s (2024) *Nature Human Behaviour* paper "High replicability of newly discovered social-behavioural findings is achievable." Verifying the preregistration against the executed study meant fighting a disorganized OSF repository ("the files are td;dr — too disorganized, didn't read"), which he synthesized into a 20+-page set of concerns upheld by a ten-month investigation. The journal retracted the paper but invited the authors to resubmit — a rare "retract-and-resubmit" — while the corresponding author publicly reframed the problems as "an embarrassing error," a narrative that scholarly news outlets echoed uncorrected.</span>[^claude-case]
