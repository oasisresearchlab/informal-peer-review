**In the preprint:** Yes — Patel & Chan (2026), §6.4.1 *Apple Cider Vinegar* (RQ4 — Impacts, Case 1).

<span style="color:orange">A viral *BMJ Nutrition, Prevention & Health* trial (Abou-Khalil et al., 2024) claimed apple cider vinegar significantly reduces weight in adolescents and young adults. Professor of Human-Computer Interaction André Calero Valdez re-analyzed the results, simulating the missing 95% confidence intervals and showing overlapping intervals — no significant intergroup weight differences — then posted in the journal's Rapid Responses and, when ignored, said so on Twitter and Bluesky; James Heathers also pressed the case on ResearchHub, and Jay Patel filed his own Rapid Response as active participant observation. About 20 months after publication the journal retracted the paper, citing flawed statistical analysis, implausible values, an unreliable dataset, inadequate reporting, missing trial registration, and irreproducible results.</span>[^claude-case]
