## 📝 Newer Codes

#inpeer/how/practices/review/paper/layers/empirical/openness/prereg/closed 
- This means that the preregistration is hard to access (gated), adding a delay.

#discussion/zoo/sciComm/biased
- This means that the individual (influencers, sci comm) reporting a study in a biased way (e.g. selectively reporting results).

#inpeer/digitalEthnography/activePartObs/tell/what/advisedCoauthor
- This means that I engaged directly with a scholar/co-author and let me know how I would have liked the research to have been designed and/or executed.

#discussion/implications/peerReview/practices/pedagogical/effectSizesClearly
- This means that the scholarship would benefit from communicating effect sizes clearly, not just with conventional notation. 

#inpeer/digitalEthnography/activePartObs/ask/community/details
- This means that I engaged in a form of active participant observation that involved requesting additional details about the study like its preregistration, data, code, materials, etc.

#inpeer/how/practices/review/paper/layers/empirical/interpretation/effectSizesDistal 
- This means that we could interpret the effect sizes presented to us more carefully by considering distal outcomes of the cause-effect claims. For example, if an intervention like UBI/cash transfer resulted in 8% high reports of having a business idea, then consider the more distal effect of actually implementing the business idea. That effect size would be lower.

#inpeer/how/practices/review/paper/layers/empirical/methods/dependentVar/chosenWrongly 
- The user critiques the use of one of more dependent variables (aka metrics, outcomes, effects). The choice of a dependent variable is crucial in a study.

#inpeer/how/practices/review/paper/layers/empirical/methods/independentVar/strengthen 
- This means that a stronger independent variable (IV) is needed. If an intervention like UBI offers $1,000/month to individuals and achieves modest outcomes, then it may be the case that a stronger benefit like $2000/month is needed to lift people out of poverty.
